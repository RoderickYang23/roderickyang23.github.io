User Persona 1

A student who is unmotivated and slacking off in a group project.
Age: 20
Role: College student
Location: Suzhou, Jiangsu
Character: Delays tasks, low engagement, easily distracted
late submission risk low initiative needs fun trigger
Goals
  • Find assignments less boring and easier to start.
  • Get interested before feeling pressured.
  • See visible rewards for small progress.
  • Turn passive participation into active contribution.
Frustrations
  • Traditional tools feel like cold checklists.
  • Long task boards create avoidance.
  • Cannot feel the impact of finishing a small task.
Motivation
Incentive
Fear
Curiosity
Social
Bio

This student is not unwilling to contribute, but usually slips into low-energy, low-focus participation. The project is designed to activate interest first: by turning tasks into quests, progress into EXP, and contribution into visible rewards, the system lowers the emotional barrier to starting work.

User Persona 2

A teacher monitoring contribution and participation
Age: 38
Role: Course instructor
Focus: Team fairness and engagement
Need: Quick evidence of who contributes more
leaderboard review fair assessment class engagement
Goals
  • Identify highly contributing students efficiently.
  • Encourage wider participation across the team.
  • Use contribution records as supporting evidence for evaluation.
Frustrations
  • Cannot easily judge invisible teamwork effort.
  • Students often claim unequal workload without proof.
  • Traditional tools show status, but not motivation quality.
Motivation
Fairness
Visibility
Efficiency
Control
Bio

The teacher needs a lightweight but convincing way to inspect team collaboration. Through the leaderboard, the teacher can directly see students with higher contribution values, making the process of supervision and formative assessment more transparent.
